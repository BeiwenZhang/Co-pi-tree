Conclusion

Co-π-tree shows that LLM reasoning can be distilled into executable and interpretable policy trees for human-AI collaboration. The learned policy executes quickly, reduces online LLM dependence, and exposes local decision branches for inspection and refinement.

Interpretability

Decision branches reveal why partner behavior is predicted and why the agent selects an action.

Efficiency

Test-time interaction avoids repeated LLM calls after the final policy tree is learned.

Collaboration

Partner prediction and partner-conditioned action selection improve coordination with AI and human partners.
